The Vision

El Nido is a new model for property ownership — one where a community of like-minded individuals holds real equity in a thriving tropical property, governed democratically through blockchain.

FAQ

Common Questions

Is this a timeshare?

No. A timeshare gives you a usage license — you pay for the right to visit but own nothing. El Nido gives you real equity: shares in Thélème S.A., a Costa Rican corporation that owns the property. You can sell your shares anytime, earn rental income, and vote on every decision.

Can I sell my shares?

Yes. Your shares can be transferred to anyone, at any time, via blockchain. There are no transfer fees, no government transfer tax, and no complex paperwork. You set your own price on the secondary market.

Do I need to be in Costa Rica?

Not at all. Many shareholders are remote investors who earn rental income from their points without ever visiting. You can manage everything remotely — voting, tracking income, and booking stays — all through our digital platform.

What if I never visit?

Your unused points are automatically made available for short-term rental. You earn a share of the rental income proportional to the points you contributed to the rental pool. Many shareholders treat this purely as an income-generating investment.

Is this regulated?

Costa Rica does not have specific fractional ownership legislation. El Nido is structured as shares in a Sociedad Anónima (S.A.), the standard Costa Rican corporate entity. All shareholder rights, governance rules, and usage policies are defined in a comprehensive operating agreement signed by all shareholders.

What are the tax implications?

Costa Rica has no capital gains tax on share sales. U.S. investors who hold more than 10% of the S.A. may need to file Form 5471 with the IRS. We recommend consulting your tax advisor for your specific situation. Rental income may be subject to Costa Rican withholding tax.

Who manages the property?

Vaillant Gicqueau, the founder, manages day-to-day operations. Vaillant also manages Jane's Beach Resort nearby, bringing years of hospitality and property management experience to El Nido. Major decisions are always made by shareholder vote.
